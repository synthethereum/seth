Project Overview & Narrative

SynthETH is a prediction-market token and protocol built on Ethereum. Users stake SynthETH into YES/NO pools on binary markets – from crypto price targets and sports events to social narratives, elections and on-chain milestones.

The imbalance between YES and NO liquidity automatically creates real-time odds. After a market is resolved using verifiable data, winning side participants receive payouts in SynthETH, turning every settled event into a redistribution of belief-weighted stakes.

The name SynthETH now stands for “Synthetic Outcomes. Real Stakes.” Instead of synthetic price-tracking assets, SynthETH powers synthetic prediction markets – markets constructed entirely from user belief, on-chain liquidity and transparent settlement.

Token Utility

1. Market Participation

Users stake SynthETH into YES or NO pools on any active market. The token is the base currency for all positions across the protocol.

2. Settlement Currency

All winning payouts are made in SynthETH, creating recurring on-chain demand whenever markets are resolved and rewards are distributed.

3. Fees & Optional Burns

A small portion of protocol fees can be directed to a burn address (if enabled by governance), introducing an optional deflationary component without changing the core token contract.

4. Governance & Parameters

Holders participate in off-chain and on-chain governance to propose and vote on:

  • new market categories and listings
  • oracle providers and dispute rules
  • fee structure and revenue sharing
  • liquidity incentives and reward schedules
  • major protocol upgrades and integrations

5. Ecosystem Alignment

SynthETH is the single asset that connects users, liquidity providers, market creators and governance participants into one aligned prediction ecosystem.

Platform Structure

A. YES/NO Market Engine

Each market consists of two on-chain liquidity pools:

  • YES Pool
  • NO Pool

Odds are derived directly from liquidity:

Odds(YES) = NO Pool / (YES + NO)
Odds(NO) = YES Pool / (YES + NO)

The more SynthETH flows into one side, the more attractive the opposing odds become. No orderbook, no off-chain matching engines – only pooled liquidity and a transparent formula.

B. Market Types

The protocol is designed for a wide spectrum of binary events, including:

  • Crypto price targets (e.g. “Will ETH close above $X on date Y?”)
  • Sports outcomes and tournament stages
  • Social/media and influencer-driven narratives
  • Election and governance events
  • On-chain metrics (TVL, volume, liquidity, address counts, etc.)

Over time, community governance can whitelist new market templates and curated feeds.

C. Settlement Phases

The protocol is intentionally staged to move from trusted to trust-minimized settlement:

  • MVP: admin-driven settlement using verifiable public sources.
  • Beta: multi-signer settlement with transparent dispute windows.
  • Full Protocol: integration with decentralized oracle providers (e.g. Chainlink, UMA) and on-chain dispute resolution.

D. On-Chain Transparency

All core components are on-chain and inspectable:

  • market creation and parameters
  • all YES/NO stakes and liquidity
  • payouts and fee flows
  • resolved outcomes and dispute actions

This makes SynthETH natively compatible with explorers, analytics dashboards, and third-party monitoring tools that can build on top of the data layer.

SynthETH – Prediction Markets Litepaper v1.0

1. Abstract

SynthETH is a fixed-supply ERC-20 token (30,000,000 units) that powers a binary prediction-market protocol on Ethereum. Users stake SynthETH into YES/NO pools on real-world and on-chain events, with odds determined by liquidity and payouts settled in SynthETH after outcomes are verified.

2. Narrative & Vision

Under the banner “Synthetic Outcomes. Real Stakes.”, SynthETH repurposes the idea of “synthetics” from price-mirroring assets to synthetic markets — markets where every event becomes a tradable binary asset constructed from user belief and on-chain liquidity.

The vision is to create a composable, transparent and high-engagement prediction layer for Ethereum, where anyone can express a view on future states of the world, and where those views are priced, traded and settled in a single, clean ERC-20 asset.

3. Core Design

  • Binary markets: each market has YES and NO pools.
  • Liquidity-defined odds: odds are a simple function of opposite-side liquidity.
  • On-chain state: stakes, outcomes and payouts are transparent and verifiable.
  • Single settlement asset: all positions and rewards use SynthETH.

This design keeps the protocol simple enough for early-stage experimentation, while being flexible enough to support advanced oracle and governance structures later on.

4. Token Specification

  • Name: SynthETH
  • Standard: ERC-20
  • Network: Ethereum Mainnet
  • Decimals: 18
  • Total Supply: 30,000,000 SynthETH (fixed)
  • Minting: none after deployment
  • Tax / Rebasing: none – clean transfer semantics

5. Token Utility

SynthETH is the native asset of the prediction protocol:

  • Staking asset: users stake SynthETH for YES/NO positions.
  • Settlement unit: winners are paid in SynthETH after markets resolve.
  • Fee unit: protocol fees are denominated and collected in SynthETH.
  • Governance: holders participate in governance over markets, parameters and incentives.

6. Tokenomics & Distribution

Total supply is allocated as follows:

  • 20,000,000 SynthETH – Permanent Lock: locked indefinitely to remove emission risk and support the fixed-supply narrative.
  • 5,000,000 SynthETH – Community Airdrop: rewards for early users, market creators and active participants across multiple campaign waves.
  • 5,000,000 SynthETH – Ecosystem & Operations: liquidity provision, protocol development, audits, oracle costs and strategic partnerships.

Circulating supply is defined as total supply minus permanently locked supply and is initially 10,000,000 SynthETH.

7. Settlement & Oracles

To balance speed and trust minimization, settlement is rolled out in stages:

  • MVP: admin-driven settlement using public, archivable sources.
  • Beta: multisig settlement with transparent dispute windows and community oversight.
  • Full protocol: integration of decentralized oracle frameworks and on-chain dispute resolution mechanisms.

The long-term goal is to minimize trust in any single entity and rely instead on economically-secured oracle systems and open challenges.

8. Roadmap (High-Level)

  • Phase 1 – MVP: token launch, core YES/NO markets, curated events, basic UI and wallet support.
  • Phase 2 – Public Beta: user-generated markets, reputation, leaderboards, fee-sharing and community campaigns.
  • Phase 3 – Oracle + Full Protocol: decentralized settlement, advanced markets, liquidity incentives and cross-chain deployment.
